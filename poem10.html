Some Burns Leave No Scars, Just Silence

When betrayal doesn’t bleed — it burns quietly, beneath the skin.

I feel my skin burn,
heat radiating through every vein,
the moment I saw you with her.

The moment I saw my everything
with a random nothing,
chills ran down my spine.

The moment you held her hand,
The moment you held her hand,
everything shattered,
everything crumbled.

But what about those promises you made?
The ones that whispered — "it's me or no one."

The promises that kept me breathing,
that kept me believing...

Until I saw you.
With her.

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem reveals the unspoken violence of heartbreak — not in wounds or words, but in silence. It explores betrayal as both fire and frost: a burn that leaves no scar yet reshapes everything within. The poem reminds us that promises can be fragile bridges, and when they collapse, what remains isn’t rage — it’s the stillness of disbelief. Through that quiet, we see the truth: not every pain announces itself; some simply hum in the silence we’re left with.
