Memories I Would Always Remember

Echoes of a love that lives on in stillness.

You came like a storm,
Wrecked me up, Left me at the bay.

And yet I am still here,
Waiting, wanting for you to come back.

The storm left, the sky's cleared.
But my heart didn't.

The silence around me echoes your voice,
As I keep on replaying it.

The laughter, the words,
And the meaning behind them.

But all of it now, is just shadows and lost warmth,
Left me cold.

And in that cold, I wrap myself in memories —
Memories of you, Of us,
Memories I would always remember.

Even if you never return,
Even if you've forgotten,
Those memories will never leave me.

As I carry them quietly,
Like the waves carry the moonlight —
Never touching, But never letting go.

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem contemplates what remains after love has passed — the afterglow of connection, preserved in fragments of memory. It reflects on how remembrance itself becomes a form of love: a silent devotion that endures without needing presence. The waves and moonlight symbolize longing and restraint — closeness that never fully meets, yet never fades. To remember, here, is to hold both grief and grace in the same tender breath.
