Wilted Petals, Silent Rain

A poem of love, loss, and the quiet ache of inevitability.

If I could turn back time
I would've never let you go

If I could turn back time
I would've held on to you as tight as possible

I would've stopped you
I would've compelled you
Maybe even threaten you

But that's not how it works, right?

So I had to let you go
I had to loosen my hold on you

And I would do anything to do otherwise

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem reflects on the ache of unrealized control — the human wish to rewrite the past, to resist endings that feel premature. Yet, beneath the longing, there’s acceptance: a realization that love’s truest form is not in possession but in surrender. Like petals in the rain, beauty remains even as it fades — fragile, fleeting, and profoundly human.
