A Season, Not a Lifetime.
But Still Remember the Warmth...

An elegy to fleeting love and the echoes it leaves behind.

Maybe it's the what ifs,
Maybe it's the maybes,
Maybe it's the "it's not you, it's me,"
Maybe it's the timing,
Maybe it's the fate... the destiny.

Maybe it's everything that went wrong,
Or maybe —
It was never meant to be right.
Never meant to last long.

Maybe it was just our delusion,
Maybe love was just a season —
A chapter filled with promises —
Promises we couldn't keep,
Promises that still echo your name,
Promises that make me miss you,
More than I want to admit.

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem explores impermanence — the truth that not all love stories are meant to endure, yet all leave traces. It accepts that some connections exist only for a season, to teach, to transform, to warm us before the cold. The “maybes” become a meditation on uncertainty — not regret, but gentle acknowledgment of fate’s design. It reminds us that love’s value isn’t measured by duration, but by the tenderness it leaves behind.
