I Still Remember It All

A tender recollection of love, loss, and the echoes that remain.

I still remember our conversations —
They are a constant ring in my ear.

I still remember our favourite place —
It is the constant peace to my eyes.

I still remember your messy hair —
My hands tangled in them while your laughter
resembling a melody.

I still remember that dimple on your right cheek —
Which is as rare as the bioluminescence.

I still remember your longing gaze —
Which got me lost every single time.

I still remember your warmth —
Wrapping me like a burrito.

I still remember your voice —
Replaying in my mind frequently.

I still remember the way you used to say my name —
Making my heart twitch and stomach tingle.

I still remember your unaligned teeth —
Which compliment you the most.

I still remember our strolls —
Making me feel you everywhere.

I still remember your cologne —
A mixture of cinnamon and sandalwood.

I still remember your handwriting —
Making me re-read our notes.

I still remember the way your eyes twinkle when you like something —
Making me want to present it right in front of you.

I still remember it all —
Every detail etched in time,
A love so vivid, so endless,
Yet now, only mine.

I still reach for echoes of you,
In the breeze... in the night —
But memories are all I hold,
Fading soft like morning light.

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem is an ode to remembrance — the act of loving through memory when presence has been lost. Every remembered detail becomes an anchor against time’s erosion, a rebellion against forgetting. It teaches that memory is both sanctuary and wound: a place where what’s gone still breathes, softly, through recollection. To remember is to love again — even in absence.
