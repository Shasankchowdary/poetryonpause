Poetry on Pause

Where words rest between silence and meaning.

-By Chakrika

Featured Poem: From a soul... to that Now stranger

I trusted you with everything-
only for you to toss it away like nothing.
I called you my confidant,
but I was only a passing name in your story.
The laughter we shared
now haunts my days, echoes through my
nights.
That friendship I thought was gold
was just a façade
a mask you wore until you got what you
wanted,
a lie I kept believing,
a dream I still wish was real.
My heart aches
to see your face and stay silent,
to watch your name blink in my inbox
and not type back,
to stare at your contact on my screen
and still not call.
Tell me
was it that easy
for you to let go?

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem reflects the pain of betrayed trust — when genuine emotion meets calculated indifference.It explores how attachment creates illusions, making lies feel like love and masks seem real.The speaker’s silence symbolizes the conflict between longing and self-respect.It questions whether emotional bonds are ever truly mutual or just projections of our own hope.Ultimately, it’s a meditation on how letting go reveals who truly cared — and who never did.
