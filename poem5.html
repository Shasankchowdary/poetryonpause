The Moon Still Waits...

A quiet confession of love that lingers beyond absence.

I didn't count the days,
just the memories — I held on.

I stayed where your presence used to feel close.

I told everyone I was fine,
but I checked for your message more than once.

You never said you would come back,
but I waited — like the moon waits for the sun,
only to realize my sun chose not to rise anymore.

If it ever chose to rise, it would know —
its moon is still up here, waiting.

And waiting.
And waiting.

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem speaks to the kind of love that doesn’t end — it simply outlives its reason. It mirrors the eternal rhythm of the moon and sun: always connected, never together. Waiting becomes both devotion and punishment, a ritual that keeps the heart alive while breaking it. In the stillness of longing, the poem finds grace — the quiet beauty of those who love even without return.
