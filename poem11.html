Loved, But Not Enough

A confession of love too gentle for a heart too distant.

I loved you with everything in me,
every pulse of my heart beating your name,
every breath holding a prayer for you.

And yet, you kicked it away —
not gently, not accidentally,
but with the force of someone
who never cared where it landed.

Tell me, how true was your *I love you*
if you could laugh without me,
smile without thinking of me,
live without knowing of me.

I am here, crying into my pillow
while you sleep in peace,
living in a world where I am nothing more
than a story you've already closed.

I loved you —
but maybe my love was too soft,
too tender for a heart like yours.

Was it you who failed to see me?
Or was it me who believed you ever did?

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem reflects the quiet devastation of unreciprocated love — the ache of giving everything and realizing it was never enough to be remembered the same way. It questions where the fault lies: in the lover who couldn’t see, or the one who loved too deeply. The truth the poem reveals is bittersweet — that love’s purity does not guarantee permanence. Still, even in heartbreak, there is dignity in feeling deeply. To love so fully, even when unseen, is its own kind of strength.
