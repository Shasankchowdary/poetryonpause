Light, Even After the Greys...

A quiet ode to resilience, to the light that learns to return.

Like the sun that rose.
like the waves that shimmer.
like the moon that shines.
like the stars that twinkle.

Here I go again —
not all light, but flickering still,
like warmth through clouds,
like laughter at dusk,
like the hush of the tide returning.

Despite all the blacks and greys,
here I am —
with golden and blue,
soft as a tide,
quiet as light returning.

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem embodies renewal — the persistence of warmth amid exhaustion. It reminds us that healing is not the sudden return of brightness, but the soft endurance of small lights: laughter, peace, memory, breath. Through its imagery of tides and celestial light, it whispers that resilience is not loud — it’s gentle, cyclical, and deeply human. Even in the greys of life, there is always a quiet shimmer waiting to rise again.
