A Poem Painted in Irises

A portrait of humanity through the silent stories of eyes.

They say eyes don't lie.

Maybe that's why I've always looked there first —
searching for truth, for stories, for pieces of people
they never meant to show.

Those brown ambered eyes shining in the sunlight.
Those deep blue eyes holding and expressing emotions deeper than the ocean.

Those cat eyes which always carry mischievous intentions in them.
Those green eyes which look deeper than the forest in the dark.

Those grey eyes sparkling despite all the greys in their lives.
Those hazel eyes, a mixture of green and brown,
seeming like a living canvas of day and night.

Those black eyes — no matter how pitch dark they are,
they never fail to speak volumes.

And in all their colors, quiet truths reside,
Soft echoes of the lives they try to hide.

No need for words, no need for sound —
Their stories in a single gaze are found.

The Philosophy Behind This Poem

This poem explores the gaze as a mirror of truth — the idea that eyes betray what words conceal. Each color becomes a metaphor for experience, emotion, and identity. It reminds us that every glance holds a universe: unspoken stories shimmering beneath silence. To see deeply is an act of compassion — and perhaps, the closest we come to understanding another soul.
